Privacy Policy

Traffic Help · Effective July 17, 2026

Traffic Help does not require an account. In the current version, route text entered in the app stays on the device and is not uploaded by us. The app uses Google services for advertising consent and rewarded ads, app integrity, crash reporting, and Google Play purchases; those services process limited technical data as described below.

1. Who we are

This policy explains how UNR Studio (“we”, “us”, or “our”) handles information when you use the Traffic Help Android application.

2. Information handled by the app

  • Route text. You may type a starting point and destination. In the current version, this text is held temporarily in app memory, is not saved to an account or database, and is not sent to our servers. It is discarded when the app process ends or you clear it.
  • Location. The current version does not request Android location permission and does not access your device’s precise location.
  • App integrity information. Firebase App Check with Google Play Integrity may process app, device, network, and integrity signals and related identifiers to verify that requests come from an authentic app installation and to protect services from abuse.
  • Crash and diagnostic information. Release versions use Firebase Crashlytics. It may process crash traces, app and OS version, device model and architecture, timestamps, installation identifiers, and related technical diagnostics. We use this information to find and fix reliability problems. Debug builds have Crashlytics collection disabled.
  • Advertising and consent information. The Google User Messaging Platform is used to request and remember advertising consent where required. If ads may be requested, the Google Mobile Ads SDK may automatically collect and share IP address (which can indicate general location), app interactions such as launches, taps and video views, diagnostic information, and device or account identifiers. Google uses this information for advertising, ad measurement, analytics, and fraud prevention. Depending on your region and choices, ads may be personalized or non-personalized.
  • Purchases. Google Play Billing processes subscription payments. We do not receive your full payment-card details. The app can receive product details, purchase status, purchase tokens, and acknowledgement status so that it can determine and provide an entitlement.

The current version does not use Firebase Authentication, Firebase Analytics, Cloud Firestore, Realtime Database, or Firebase Storage, and does not create user profiles.

3. Why information is used

Information is processed only as needed to:

  • operate the app and provide requested features;
  • protect the app and backend services from fraud, abuse, and unauthorized clients;
  • diagnose crashes and improve stability;
  • request and respect advertising consent and provide rewarded ads;
  • process and restore Google Play purchases; and
  • comply with legal obligations and enforce applicable terms.

Where applicable, our legal bases include performance of a contract, our legitimate interests in security and reliability, compliance with law, and your consent for advertising activities that require consent. You may withdraw advertising consent through available consent controls; withdrawal does not affect processing that occurred before withdrawal.

4. Service providers and sharing

We do not sell your personal information. Information may be processed by Google LLC and its affiliates through Firebase App Check, Firebase Crashlytics, Google Play Integrity, Google Mobile Ads, the User Messaging Platform, and Google Play Billing. Google may act as our service provider or as an independent controller depending on the service and context.

  • Google Privacy Policy
  • Firebase privacy and security information
  • Google Mobile Ads data disclosure
  • How Google uses information for advertising

We may also disclose information if required by law, to protect users or the public, or as part of a business transfer subject to appropriate safeguards.

5. Retention and deletion

  • Route text is not retained by us in the current version.
  • Firebase states that Crashlytics retains crash traces and associated installation identifiers for 90 days before beginning removal from live and backup systems.
  • App Check, advertising, consent, and purchase information is retained by Google according to the applicable Google terms, user settings, and legal requirements.

You can stop future app collection by uninstalling the app. You can also clear the app’s local data in Android settings, reset or delete your Android advertising ID, manage advertising choices in My Ad Center, and manage subscriptions in Google Play. To request deletion of information that we control, contact us using the address below. We may need limited information to verify and complete a request.

6. Security and international processing

We use reasonable technical and organizational safeguards. Network communication with Firebase and Google services is encrypted in transit, and Firebase states that App Check and Crashlytics data are encrypted at rest. No method of transmission or storage is completely secure. Google and its providers may process information in countries other than your own, subject to their legal transfer mechanisms and safeguards.

7. Your rights and choices

Depending on where you live, you may have rights to access, correct, delete, restrict, object to, or receive a copy of personal information, and to withdraw consent. You may also have the right to complain to your local data-protection authority. Contact us to exercise rights concerning information we control. Requests concerning Google-controlled data may also be handled through your Google account and Google’s privacy tools.

8. Children

Traffic Help is not directed to children under 13 or the minimum digital-consent age in their country. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided personal information, contact us so that we can investigate and take appropriate action. The app must be reconfigured and this policy updated before it is intentionally offered to children.

9. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy when the app, its service providers, or legal requirements change. We will publish the revised policy with a new effective date. Material changes will be communicated through the app or store listing when appropriate.
